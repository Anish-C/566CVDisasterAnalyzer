STEP 5: PREPARE FOR Q&A (5 minutes)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

LIKELY QUESTIONS & ANSWERS:

Q: "Why use pre-trained ImageNet weights instead of fine-tuning on disaster data?"
A: "Time constraint—we focused on demonstrating the integration of CV with 
   optimization. A production system would fine-tune on xBD's 850K labeled buildings 
   for better accuracy."

Q: "How accurate is your damage classification?"
A: "ResNet-50 shows ~25-35% uncertainty across damage classes on real hurricane 
   imagery. This uncertainty is exactly what our optimization accounts for."

Q: "How did you get 2000 labeled images?"
A: "These are real hurricane satellite images from Kaggle with damage labels. 
   They represent actual hurricane-affected areas."

Q: "Can this actually be deployed?"
A: "Yes, this is proof-of-concept. The three-stage pipeline is production-ready 
   if we had more time to fine-tune on xBD and test with real solvers like Gurobi."

Q: "What's the most important contribution?"
A: "Showing that prediction uncertainty is actionable information for optimization. 
   Most disaster systems ignore uncertainty; we use it to make fundamentally better 
   allocation decisions."

Q: "How much improvement is realistic?"
A: "Our 16% improvement is on synthetic uncertainty patterns. Real-world improvement 
   would depend on actual CV performance. The framework adapts to whatever 
   uncertainty the CV system has."
